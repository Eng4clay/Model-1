Compliance Note:

Reference: ASHRAE Standard 170-2021

In accordance with ASHRAE 170‑2021, positive pressure differential shall be maintained at a minimum of +2.5 Pa relative to adjacent areas. While no absolute maximum is mandated, maintaining an upper limit of +6 Pa is considered best engineering practice to ensure door operability and effective airflow containment. The facility adopts 2.5 – 6 Pa as its operational benchmark.

Key Formulas for Field Calculation

1. Air Changes per Hour (ACH)

\[ ACH = \frac{Q}{V} \]

Where:
· \( Q \) = Supply airflow rate (m³/h)
· \( V \) = Room volume (m³) = Length × Width × Height

2. Airflow Rate from Velocity (if hood not used)

\[ Q = A \times v \times 3600 \]

Where:
· \( A \) = Diffuser free area (m²)
· \( v \) = Measured air velocity (m/s)

3. Differential Pressure (from velocity, if needed)

\[ \Delta P = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^{2} \]

Where:
· \( \rho \approx 1.2\ \text{kg/m}^3 \) (air density at standard conditions)
· \( v \) = Air velocity (m/s)

Key Terminology

  • Differential Pressure (ΔP) – Pascals (Pa)
  • Air Changes per Hour (ACH)

Action Protocol

  • Verify pressure weekly when room is occupied.
  • Log all readings and report deviations to Utilities & Maintenance (U&M) via work order.
  • Notify IPC immediately if pressure falls below +2.5 Pa or reverses.
